Course Goal

This course gives students an understanding of modern approaches for analyzing the structure and function of the brain. Through hands-on work with real neuroscience data, students learn to map the brain using contemporary neuroanatomical techniques and to interpret its genetic and functional organization.

Modules

Module 1: The Functional Neuroanatomy of the Human Brain

March 31 – May 1

Map the brain using transcriptomics from the Allen Institute for Brain Science, white matter connectomics, and fMRI data compiled from thousands of studies. Understand the genetic and functional organization of human brain regions.

Practicum prompts due: April 10  ·  Practicum due: April 20
▶ Go to Module 1 Practicum

Module 2: The Neurophysiology of the Human Brain

May 5 – June 8

Non-invasively record and analyze EEG (electroencephalogram) signals from the human brain and compare them to ongoing behavior of subjects.

Practicum prompts due: May 18  ·  Practicum due: June 8 (Monday of finals week)
