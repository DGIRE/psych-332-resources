Electrooculography (EOG)

Learn to record eye signals using high-quality patch electrodes and Backyard Brains technology.

The Eye as a Dipole

The eye is effectively a biological battery. The front (the cornea) is more positively charged than the back (the retina).

This creates an electric dipole. When you move your eyes, you rotate this dipole, shifting the electric field around your head. Adhesive patch electrodes allow us to capture these subtle voltage changes.

"EOG measures the corneo-retinal standing potential. This signal typically ranges from 0.01 to 0.1 mV."

Setup Walkthrough

Step 1

Lateral Electrode Placement

Stick two adhesive patch electrodes on the outer temples, one on the left side and one on the right side of your eyes.

Step 2

Place the Ground

Place the third patch electrode on the mastoid process—the bony bump directly behind your ear.

Step 3

Connect Alligator Clips

Clip the two red alligator clips to the electrodes by your eyes. Clip the black cable to the ground electrode behind your ear.

System Active! 🚀

Connect via USB in SpikeRecorder. Look far left, then far right to see the signal deflect.

Expert Troubleshooting

⚡ Signal Noise

  • Unplug your laptop: Run on battery to eliminate 60Hz wall interference.
  • Skin Prep: Ensure the skin is clean and dry before applying patches.
  • Notch Filter: Use the 60 Hz filter (Americas) or 50 Hz (Europe) in SpikeRecorder.

👁️ Vertical Eye Movements

To measure up/down movements or blinks, place one patch above the eye and one below. Note that blinks will show up as very sharp spikes because of the muscle activity in your eyelids.
