Fundamentals of Electrophysiological Data Acquisition

What this video covers: An introduction to the principles and methods of electrophysiological data acquisition. Topics include how electrical signals are generated by neural populations, the physical basis of EEG and ECoG recordings, electrode placement and referencing schemes, amplification and digitization of neural signals, and common sources of noise and artifact. This video provides the conceptual foundation needed before working with electrophysiological data in any analysis context.

Tutorial Video

EEG Frequency Bands

What this video covers: An overview of the canonical EEG frequency bands and their functional significance. The video introduces the defining frequency ranges for delta (<4 Hz), theta (4–8 Hz), alpha (8–13 Hz), beta (13–30 Hz), and gamma (>30 Hz) oscillations, and explains what each band reflects in terms of brain state and cognitive function. Understanding these bands is essential for interpreting the spectral features you will compute in the Module 2 practicum.
