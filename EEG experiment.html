Exploring the Frontal Lobe

Basics of EEG and Fp1/Fp2 Recording with Backyard Brains

What is an EEG?

Electroencephalography (EEG) measures the electrical activity of your brain. When neurons fire, they create tiny electrical impulses. By placing electrodes on the scalp, we can detect the synchronized activity of millions of neurons.

"The signal we measure is in the range of 10s of microvolts (μV). That's 100 times smaller than your heart signal (EKG)!"

Why Fp1 and Fp2?

The Frontal Pole (Fp) electrodes sit directly over your prefrontal cortex. This area is responsible for:

  • Executive Function
  • Blinking/Eye Movement
  • Concentration

Setup: Backyard Brains Heart & Brain SpikerBox

We will be using adhesive electrode patches on the forehead. This provides a cleaner signal than the headband for frontal lobe recordings.

Step 1

Clean the Forehead

Use an alcohol wipe or soapy water to clean the skin over your eyebrows. Skin oils are "insulators" and will block the signal!

Step 2

Placement (Fp1/Fp2)

Place two adhesive electrodes on your forehead, approximately 1 inch above the center of each eyebrow. These are your active channels.

Step 3

The Ground

Place the third electrode on the Mastoid Process (the bony part behind either ear). This is critical to reduce electrical noise.

Connecting the Leads

Red Clips: Attach to the two electrodes on your forehead (Fp1 & Fp2).

Black Clip: Attach to the electrode behind your ear (Ground).

Orange Cable: Plug into the orange port on the SpikerBox.

Troubleshooting Noise

  • No Signal? Make sure the SpikerBox is turned ON (red switch) and the 9V battery is fresh.
  • 60Hz Hum? If the signal looks like a thick solid wall of noise, turn on the 60Hz Notch Filter in settings.
  • Muscle Artifact? Relax your face! Jaw clenching creates massive EMG signals.
